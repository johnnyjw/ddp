This Shiny Application allows users to visualize volcanic eruption data and get insights to where different regions of the world have had many volcanic eruptions.

The Data

  • This dataset comes from the National Geophysical Data Center / World Data Service.
  • Each row in the dataset is a Country/Region, a year, and the number of significant volcano eruptions in that year.
  • A significant eruption is classified as one that meets at least one of the following criteria: caused fatalities, caused moderate damage (approximately $1 million or more), with a Volcanic Explosivity Index (VEI) of 6 or larger, caused a tsunami, or was associated with a major earthquake.
  • The timespan of the data is from 1750 BCE to 2017 AD.
  • The completeness of the data for earlier years is expected to be very low.

The Application

What does the application do?

  • Shows the total number of Significant Volcanic eruptions by Country/Region or by Continent and see where most of the eruptions have occured.
  • Allows the user to control the timespan for which the total number of Significant Volcanic Eruptions are calculated.
  • Allows the user to limit the top number of countries/regions or continents to display on the graph and table.

Why is this useful?

  • Allows the user to get quick insights into where in the world there are volcanic eruptions
  • How incomplete is earlier data?
  • Have the same parts of the world consistently had the most eruptions across time?
  • Where have the most eruptions occurred during certain timespans?
